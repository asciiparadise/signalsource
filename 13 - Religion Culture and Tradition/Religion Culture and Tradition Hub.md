# Religion, Culture and Tradition Hub

## Purpose

This section is for the world's religions, belief systems, rituals, cultural traditions and inherited symbolic structures.

It needs to be handled carefully.

Signal Source is not trying to flatten living religions into aesthetic material. The point is not to turn Buddhism, Christianity, Islam, Hinduism, Judaism, Indigenous traditions or folk belief into a moodboard.

The point is to understand how belief becomes culture, how culture becomes ritual, how ritual becomes architecture, music, image, morality, timekeeping and everyday life.

## Working Principle

Every religion should be treated on two levels:

1. **Living tradition** — real communities, practices, histories and beliefs.
2. **Symbolic influence** — how the tradition appears in art, music, film, architecture, myth and Signal Source themes.

Those two levels should not be confused.

## Why It Belongs Here

Religion is one of the oldest world-building systems humans have.

It gives form to time, death, ethics, food, sex, family, architecture, sound, colour, clothing, language, law and the body.

That is why it belongs in Signal Source.

Not as exotic material.

As one of the deepest structures through which humans make meaning.

## Signal Source Reading

Belief is not only something people privately hold in their heads.

It becomes visible.

A temple.

A calendar.

A song.

A gesture.

A fast.

A burial.

A symbol.

A city.

Religion matters here because it shows how invisible conviction becomes shared form.
