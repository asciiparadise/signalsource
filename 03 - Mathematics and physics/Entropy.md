# Entropy

Entropy is the slow movement toward disorder, decay, heat, exhaustion and dispersal.

In Signal Source it matters both scientifically and emotionally.

Scientifically, entropy belongs to physics and the direction of time. Emotionally, it describes the feeling that systems do not stay whole forever. Bodies fail. tapes degrade. buildings empty out. futures collapse into maintenance, warning and dust.

That is probably why it appears so often in the works collected here.

## Why It Matters

Entropy gives time a texture.

It is not just that things end. It is that they wear down. They lose sharpness. They blur, fade, crack, repeat badly, overheat, collapse into noise.

That is very close to the emotional world of Boards of Canada, William Basinski, Anselm Kiefer and Twin Peaks.

## Signal Source Reading

Entropy is frightening because it is not dramatic.

It does not always arrive as disaster. Sometimes it arrives as fading colour, tape wobble, empty roads, failed infrastructure, and the feeling that the future is still technically running but no longer believes in itself.
