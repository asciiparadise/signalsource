# Numbers

Numbers in Signal Source are never only numbers.

They are counting, order, childhood, control, mathematics, ritual, codes, release dates, track lengths, coordinates, frequencies and the strange comfort of believing that the world can be arranged.

Boards of Canada understand this very well.

A number can feel innocent when a child is counting. It can feel scientific when attached to frequency or astronomy. It can feel occult when placed inside a pattern. It can feel paranoid when repeated too often.

That movement is the interesting part.

## Childhood and Order

Counting is one of the earliest ways we learn structure.

Before mathematics becomes abstract, it is physical and rhythmic. One, two, three. Steps, claps, games, voices, loops.

That is why numbers in Boards of Canada can feel both playful and unsettling. They return us to something basic, but they also suggest systems we do not fully understand.

## Codes and Hidden Systems

Numbers easily become clues.

Track lengths, frequencies, dates, ratios, coordinates, samples, catalogue numbers. Sometimes they really matter. Sometimes they probably do not. But they create a state of attention where the listener becomes alert to structure.

That is where numbers connect to [[Pattern Recognition]].
