# Dante - Purgatorio

## Author

Dante Alighieri

## Medium

Epic poem / ascent / purification structure

## Why It Belongs Here

*Purgatorio* belongs in Signal Source because it is about transformation rather than damnation.

After the descent of [[Dante - Inferno]], this part begins the climb. That shift matters. The landscape is still symbolic, still moral, still structured, but the movement is different. Hell is enclosure. Purgatory is process.

It is one of the great works about becoming capable of change.

## What We Actually Know

*Purgatorio* is the second part of *The Divine Comedy*.

Dante and Virgil climb Mount Purgatory, where souls undergo purification before entering Paradise. The mountain is organised into terraces, each associated with a form of sin being corrected or transformed.

Unlike Hell, Purgatory is temporary. Suffering here has direction.

## Ascent as Structure

The central symbolic movement is upward.

That sounds simple, but it changes everything. The soul is still marked by sin and damage, but it is not fixed forever. It can be disciplined, reoriented, cleansed and prepared.

In Signal Source terms, *Purgatorio* is about ritual passage.

A person moves through structured difficulty in order to become able to see differently.

## Signal Source Reading

*Purgatorio* matters because it gives suffering a different meaning from *Inferno*.

In Hell, suffering confirms the soul's condition.

In Purgatory, suffering changes it.

That is why this part is important for the vault's interest in ritual, initiation, transformation and altered states. It suggests that symbolic architecture does not only trap. It can also remake.
