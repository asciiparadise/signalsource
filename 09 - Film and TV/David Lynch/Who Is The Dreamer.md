# Who Is The Dreamer?

## Type
Core Mystery

## Source

Monica Bellucci's dream conversation with Gordon Cole.

## The Question

> We are like the dreamer who dreams and then lives inside the dream.
>
> But who is the dreamer?

This may be the central question of Twin Peaks.

## Interpretation 1: Cooper

Cooper increasingly behaves like a dreamer attempting to shape reality.

Part 18 suggests he may have entered a dream-like construction of reality and lost control of it.

## Interpretation 2: Laura Palmer

Twin Peaks begins and ends with Laura.

One reading suggests the entire mythology is organised around her trauma, memory and psychic reality.

Carrie Page may be Laura dreaming another life.

## Interpretation 3: Audrey Horne

Audrey's storyline resembles a dream loop or enclosed reality.

Her final appearance suggests awakening from an artificial world.

## Interpretation 4: David Lynch and the Audience

On a meta-level, Lynch himself is the dreamer.

The audience also becomes a dreamer by inhabiting the fictional world.

## Signal Source Interpretation

The brilliance of the question lies in its refusal to settle.

The dreamer can be:

- Cooper
- Laura
- Audrey
- Lynch
- the audience

All at once.

## Vault Interpretation

The question matters because it shifts attention away from plot and toward ontology.

Not:

What happened?

But:

What is reality?
