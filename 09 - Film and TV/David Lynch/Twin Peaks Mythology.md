# Twin Peaks Mythology

## Core Idea

The mythology of Twin Peaks is not a system designed to be solved.

It behaves more like folklore, dreams, religion and myth.

The purpose is not explanation. The purpose is resonance.

## The Forest

The forest is the first mythic structure.

It exists simultaneously as:

- wilderness
- unconscious mind
- spiritual threshold
- hidden America

Like the Zone in Stalker, it is a place where ordinary rules weaken.

## The Lodges

The Black Lodge and White Lodge should not be understood as simple heaven and hell.

They are symbolic territories.

They resemble:

- dream worlds
- spiritual dimensions
- psychic states
- mythic landscapes

## The Threshold

One of the recurring ideas in Twin Peaks is crossing a threshold.

Red curtains, hallways, roads, forests, electricity, dreams and mirrors all function as gateways.

## Time

Time in Twin Peaks is unstable.

Past, present and future bleed together.

Characters often experience reality as though it were already remembered.

## The Mythic Function

Twin Peaks turns modern America into folklore.

The diner, the forest, the road, the motel, the sheriff station and the living room become equivalent to castles, sacred groves and mythic kingdoms.
