# The Big Bang

## Why It Belongs Here

The Big Bang belongs in Signal Source because it is the modern creation myth, even when treated scientifically.

That does not mean it is only a myth. It is the dominant scientific model for the early development of the universe. But culturally, it also occupies the place older creation stories once occupied: the question of how everything began.

The strange thing is that the scientific version may be even more overwhelming than the mythic one.

No garden.

No gods shaping clay.

Just an unimaginably dense, hot beginning, expansion, cooling, matter, stars, galaxies, and eventually consciousness trying to understand where it came from.

## What We Actually Know

The Big Bang model describes the universe expanding from an extremely hot, dense early state roughly 13.8 billion years ago.

Important evidence includes:

- the expansion of the universe
- the cosmic microwave background
- the abundance of light elements
- large-scale cosmic structure

It is not an explosion into empty space. It is the expansion of space itself.

The model explains a great deal, but it does not answer every question. It does not simply explain why there is something rather than nothing.

## Symbolic Role

In Signal Source, the Big Bang matters because it connects science, awe, time and origin.

It is the point where physics becomes almost impossible to separate from metaphysical wonder.

Everything that appears later in the vault — music, memory, bodies, symbols, grief, tape, dreams, black holes, consciousness — exists downstream from that first cosmic expansion.

## Signal Source Reading

The Big Bang is not only a scientific topic here.

It is a scale shift.

It forces the vault to remember that all the intimate things it studies — a record, a photograph, a childhood memory, a heartbeat — are happening inside a universe with a history almost too large to emotionally process.

That tension is useful.

The cosmic and the personal should not be separated too cleanly.
