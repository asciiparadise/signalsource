# Operation Northwoods

## Evidence Status

**Documented proposal / not executed.**

Operation Northwoods should be treated carefully.

It was a real 1962 proposal developed within the U.S. Department of Defense / Joint Chiefs context for false-flag-style pretexts against Cuba. It was not carried out, and President John F. Kennedy did not approve it.

That distinction matters.

The proposal is documented.

The events did not happen.

## Why It Belongs Here

Operation Northwoods belongs in Signal Source because it shows why some kinds of political paranoia have a factual root.

It is not evidence that every false-flag theory is true.

But it is evidence that state planners could formally imagine extreme deception as policy: staged attacks, manipulated evidence, and public justification for war.

That is enough to damage trust.

## What We Actually Know

The proposal involved scenarios that would create or simulate hostile Cuban aggression in order to build support for military action against Cuba.

Suggested ideas included staged incidents, fake attacks and deception operations.

The plan remained a proposal and was rejected.

## What Is Mythology

The mythology begins when Northwoods is used as a universal key.

Because one extreme false-flag proposal existed, some conspiracy thinking treats every later crisis as secretly staged.

That is not a responsible conclusion.

The correct lesson is narrower but still disturbing: institutions can produce documents that imagine immoral actions, even when those actions are not carried out.

## Signal Source Reading

Operation Northwoods matters because it reveals the imaginative life of state power.

Not only what the state does.

What it is capable of proposing.

That is a different kind of horror: the archive as evidence of possibilities that were considered, written down, circulated and then buried.
