# Operation Paperclip

## Evidence Status

**Documented postwar intelligence and recruitment programme.**

Operation Paperclip is documented history, not conspiracy folklore.

The surrounding mythology can become exaggerated, but the central fact is real: after the Second World War, the United States brought German scientists, engineers and technicians, including people with Nazi affiliations, into American programmes for military, scientific and strategic purposes.

## Why It Belongs Here

Operation Paperclip belongs in Signal Source because it is one of the clearest examples of moral compromise being absorbed into the machinery of progress.

The space age, weapons development, Cold War competition and scientific ambition did not emerge from a clean ethical vacuum. They were built partly through decisions made in the shadow of catastrophe.

That makes Paperclip important for the vault.

It shows how the future can be built from contaminated material.

## What We Actually Know

Operation Paperclip was a U.S. programme that recruited German scientists and technical specialists after World War II.

The motivation was partly strategic: prevent expertise from falling into Soviet hands and use German scientific knowledge for American military, aerospace and technological development.

Some individuals had Nazi Party affiliations or links to wartime systems, and records were sometimes sanitised or managed to make their integration more acceptable.

## What Is Mythology

The mythology around Paperclip often expands into the idea that Nazi ideology secretly took over American institutions wholesale.

That is too broad and usually unsupported.

But the documented history is disturbing enough without exaggeration: a victorious state decided that technical expertise could outweigh moral contamination.
