# Dulce Base Myth

## Evidence Status

**Conspiracy folklore / alien-underground-base mythology.**

The Dulce Base myth should not be treated as documented history.

It belongs in Signal Source because of its mythic structure and cultural role, not because its central claims are established fact.

## Why It Belongs Here

The Dulce Base myth belongs here because it is one of the purest forms of underground-base paranoia.

A secret facility beneath a mesa.

Government-alien collaboration.

Genetic experiments.

Levels descending into the earth.

A violent firefight underground.

It combines Cold War secrecy, UFO mythology, military black projects, subterranean horror and the ancient symbolic fear of what lies beneath.

## What We Actually Know

Dulce, New Mexico is a real place.

Archuleta Mesa is a real landscape feature.

The alleged underground alien-government facility, however, is part of conspiracy lore and has not been established as fact.

## What Is Mythology

The myth is powerful because it uses a very old structure in modern clothing.

Instead of a cave full of spirits, we get a base full of aliens.

Instead of forbidden underworld descent, we get security levels beneath a mesa.

Instead of demons, we get non-human intelligences and black-budget science.

The story is modern folklore with military aesthetics.

## Signal Source Reading

Dulce matters because it shows how the underworld returns in technological form.

The myth is not credible as evidence, but it is revealing as imagination.

It asks what people fear is happening beneath the surface of official reality: experiments, alliances, bodies, secrecy, violence, hidden knowledge.

That makes it a cousin of [[Montauk Project]], [[Area 51 and Project Aquatone]] and [[The Ninth Gate]], though with a much more pulp-conspiracy texture.
