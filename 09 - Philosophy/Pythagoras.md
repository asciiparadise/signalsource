# Pythagoras

## Why He Belongs Here

Pythagoras belongs in Signal Source because he sits at the point where number, music, geometry and cosmic order begin to merge.

Even if the historical Pythagoras is difficult to separate from legend, the Pythagorean idea is extremely important: reality may be structured by number.

That one thought runs through a huge part of the vault.

It connects musical intervals, sacred geometry, time signatures, the hexagon, the Music of the Spheres, and the feeling that mathematics is not just a tool humans use, but something built into the world.

## What We Actually Know

Pythagoras was an ancient Greek thinker associated with mathematics, music theory and a religious-philosophical community.

The Pythagorean tradition is strongly associated with the discovery that musical intervals can be expressed through simple numerical ratios.

For example:

- octave: 2:1
- perfect fifth: 3:2
- perfect fourth: 4:3

These relationships helped create one of the oldest bridges between music and mathematics.

## Signal Source Reading

Pythagoras matters less as a biography and more as a symbolic hinge.

He gives the vault a way to connect:

- music
- number
- geometry
- cosmos

That is almost too perfect for Signal Source.

The danger is always over-mystification. Not every number is sacred. Not every pattern means something.

But the Pythagorean instinct remains powerful: when sound becomes ratio, and ratio becomes beauty, it is hard not to wonder whether the universe itself is musical in some deeper sense.
