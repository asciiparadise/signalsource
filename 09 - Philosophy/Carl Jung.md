# Carl Jung

## Why He Belongs Here

Carl Jung belongs in Signal Source because he gives language to something the vault keeps circling: symbols are not only decorations.

They act on us.

Dreams, myths, shadows, doubles, houses, forests, mothers, masks, spirals, monsters. Jung matters because he treats these not as random images, but as psychic structures that return across cultures and individuals.

That does not mean Jung should be treated as unquestionable truth. Some of his ideas are speculative, and some need caution. But as a symbolic framework for art, myth and dream logic, he is almost unavoidable here.

## What We Actually Know

Jung was a Swiss psychiatrist and founder of analytical psychology.

Key ideas associated with him include:

- the unconscious
- the collective unconscious
- archetypes
- the shadow
- individuation
- anima and animus
- synchronicity
- dreams as symbolic communication

His work sits somewhere between psychology, mythology, religion and philosophy, which is exactly why it can be both useful and risky.

## Signal Source Reading

Jung is useful because he respects the intelligence of images.

A dream image may not be literal, but it may still know something. A symbol may not have one fixed meaning, but it may still carry force. A shadow self may not be supernatural, but it may still behave like a demon when denied.

That is why he belongs here.

He helps the vault ask what symbols do before we reduce them to what they mean.
