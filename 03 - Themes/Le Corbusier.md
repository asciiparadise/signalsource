# Le Corbusier

## Why He Belongs Here

Le Corbusier belongs in Signal Source because he represents modern architecture at its most visionary and most troubling.

He believed architecture could reorganise life. Cities, homes, furniture, streets, light, air, proportion and daily routines could be redesigned according to a new modern logic.

That ambition is fascinating.

It is also dangerous.

Signal Source is interested in exactly this kind of tension: when order becomes beauty, and when order becomes control.

## What We Actually Know

Le Corbusier was a Swiss-French architect, designer, urban planner and theorist, one of the most influential figures in modern architecture.

He is associated with:

- modernism
- reinforced concrete
- pilotis
- roof gardens
- open floor plans
- horizontal windows
- free façades
- urban planning
- the Modulor proportional system

Important works include:

- Villa Savoye
- Unité d’Habitation
- Notre-Dame du Haut at Ronchamp
- Chandigarh planning work

## The House as Machine

Le Corbusier famously described the house as a machine for living in.

That phrase can sound cold, but it also captures the modernist dream: architecture should work. It should serve life efficiently, rationally and beautifully.

But the phrase also reveals the problem.

When life is treated too much like a system, the human messiness of living can disappear.

## Proportion and the Modulor

The Modulor is especially relevant to Signal Source.

Le Corbusier developed it as a proportional system based on the human body, mathematics and the golden ratio. It was an attempt to connect architecture, measure, beauty and embodied experience.

This links directly to:

- [[Golden Ratio]]
- [[Mathematics and Music]]
- [[Sacred Geometry]]
- [[Design Theory]]

Again, the question is double-edged.

Can number humanise space?

Or does it risk turning the body into a unit of design control?

## Urban Vision and Control

Le Corbusier's urban planning ideas are where the tension becomes most severe.

His visions of towers, open space, circulation and rational city planning helped shape modern architecture and planning, but they also influenced environments that many later experienced as alienating or over-controlled.

This is where he connects to [[Brutalism]], [[Lost Futures]] and [[Architecture as Power]].

## Signal Source Reading

Le Corbusier matters because he shows modernism as both promise and threat.

The promise: light, air, order, proportion, clarity, better living.

The threat: abstraction, control, standardisation, the city treated as a machine.

He belongs in the vault because he makes architecture philosophical. A building is no longer only a shelter. It becomes an argument about what a human being is.
