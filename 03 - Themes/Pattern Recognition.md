# Pattern Recognition

Pattern recognition is one of the central forces in Signal Source.

It is the thing that lets a hexagon in a carpet suddenly feel connected to a record, a wasp nest, a logo, a film, a piece of sacred geometry and some half-remembered statement about raw mathematics underneath reality.

Sometimes pattern recognition reveals something real.

Sometimes it creates meaning where there may only be coincidence.

Most of the interesting art in this vault lives in that uncertain space.

## Why It Matters

Boards of Canada, Twin Peaks, cult imagery, sacred geometry and conspiracy culture all depend on pattern recognition.

They make the viewer or listener feel that the visible surface is not the whole thing. There is another layer. Maybe symbolic. Maybe psychological. Maybe mathematical. Maybe invented by us while we look.

That uncertainty is the point.

## Healthy and Unhealthy Forms

Pattern recognition can be creative.

It can connect works, memories and symbols in useful ways.

But it can also become paranoid. Everything becomes evidence. Every date, every shape, every phrase becomes part of a total system.

Signal Source should stay aware of both sides.
