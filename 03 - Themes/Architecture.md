# Architecture

## Why It Belongs Here

Architecture belongs in Signal Source because space is never neutral.

A building can shelter, intimidate, guide, conceal, stage rituals, enforce power, preserve memory or make a person feel small. Architecture gives ideas a body. It turns belief, money, state power, fear, worship and domestic life into physical form.

That is why it connects so naturally to *Blade Runner*, *House of Leaves*, *Twin Peaks*, Anselm Kiefer, brutalism, occult temples, public institutions and abandoned military sites.

A building is never only a building here.

It is a psychological instrument.

## What We Actually Know

Architecture is the design and construction of built environments: houses, temples, cities, monuments, factories, bunkers, stations, towers, museums, offices and ruins.

In Signal Source, the focus is less on architectural history as a complete discipline and more on how architecture produces atmosphere and meaning.

Important areas:

- scale
- threshold
- circulation
- symmetry
- monumentality
- material
- light
- shadow
- decay
- power
- domesticity
- sacred space
- urban density

## Architecture as Psyche

Some works make architecture behave like consciousness.

The house in [[House of Leaves]] is the obvious example: it is larger inside than outside, and its impossible rooms seem to externalise trauma, obsession and fear.

But the same idea appears elsewhere. The Red Room in [[Twin Peaks Mythology]] is not just a room. It is a psychic and cosmic threshold. The city in [[Blade Runner Universe]] is not only a city. It is a degraded memory-machine made of rain, neon and corporate power.

Architecture can become the shape of a mind.

## Architecture as Power

Monumental architecture often reveals what a culture worships.

Corporate towers, fascist halls, churches, brutalist institutions, bunkers, surveillance spaces and shopping malls all organise bodies differently.

This is why [[Anselm Kiefer]] matters so much. His ruined interiors and memory chambers show how architecture can survive after the belief system behind it has become poisonous.

## Architecture as Ruin

Ruins are architecture after certainty has failed.

A ruin has lost its original function, but gained memory. It no longer works in the practical sense, but it may speak more clearly than when it was new.

That connects architecture to [[Decay]], [[Entropy]], [[Ruins]], [[The Rings of Saturn]] and [[Tomorrow's Harvest]].

## Signal Source Reading

Architecture matters because it makes invisible systems visible.

A belief becomes a temple.

A corporation becomes a tower.

A trauma becomes a hallway.

A state becomes a bunker.

A memory becomes a ruin.

This is why architecture should be treated as one of the vault's core interpretive fields, not just a background category.
