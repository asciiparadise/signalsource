# Design Theory

## Why It Belongs Here

Design theory belongs in Signal Source because the vault is not only about works of art.

It is also about how worlds are built.

A record sleeve, a website, a film title card, a magazine spread, a fictional archive, a logo, a colour palette, a typographic system — all of these can carry meaning before a single sentence is read.

Design is not decoration.

Design is how attention is organised.

## What We Actually Know

Design theory is a broad field concerned with visual communication, form, function, hierarchy, composition, typography, colour, materiality, usability and meaning.

Important ideas include:

- hierarchy
- contrast
- balance
- rhythm
- grid systems
- typography
- composition
- affordance
- visual identity
- materiality
- semiotics
- user experience

In Signal Source, design theory should not become a generic design-school section. It should focus on how design creates atmosphere, belief, memory and symbolic coherence.

## Design as World-Building

The most interesting design does not only make something look good.

It makes a world feel internally real.

Boards of Canada artwork, Kate Gibb's print work, Big Active's identity systems, Robert Beatty's surreal record sleeves, *Blade Runner* production design, old public-information films, cult documents, occult diagrams — all of these use design to create a belief environment.

That is the useful phrase here:

belief environment.

A visual system can make the viewer feel that a larger world exists outside the frame.

## Imperfection and Materiality

Signal Source is especially interested in design that carries traces of process:

- worn print
- photocopy grain
- VHS damage
- misregistration
- analogue colour drift
- screen texture
- paper aging
- crude diagrams
- institutional forms
- archival labels

This is not nostalgia by itself.

It is the feeling that design has passed through time.

## Signal Source Reading

Design theory matters because it explains how atmosphere becomes structured.

A symbol repeated often enough becomes identity.

A colour palette becomes a world.

A typeface becomes a voice.

A grid becomes authority.

A damaged image becomes memory.

That is why design belongs here. It is the practical craft behind many of the vault's larger obsessions.
