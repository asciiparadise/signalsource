# Mathematics and Music

## Type
Theme

## Core Idea

Music is organised time, and mathematics is one of the main ways humans describe pattern, proportion, repetition and structure.

This does not mean that all music is secretly mathematical in a mystical sense. It means that music naturally produces mathematical relationships: pitch ratios, rhythm cycles, harmonic intervals, phrase lengths, tuning systems, repetition, symmetry, recursion and proportion.

## Main Areas

### Pitch Ratios

Musical intervals can be described as frequency ratios.

Simple ratios often sound stable or consonant:

- octave: 2:1
- perfect fifth: 3:2
- perfect fourth: 4:3
- major third: 5:4

This is one reason music has long been connected to number, harmony and cosmic order.

### Rhythm and Counting

Rhythm is mathematics made bodily.

It includes:

- pulses
- subdivisions
- polyrhythms
- odd meters
- phrase lengths
- cycles
- repetition with variation

A 7/8 pattern, a 5/4 loop or a polymetric sequence creates a felt mathematical structure before it becomes a consciously understood one.

### Form and Proportion

Composition can use proportion to shape time.

A track may place a change, break, climax or transition at a mathematically significant point:

- midpoint
- thirds
- golden ratio point
- Fibonacci-derived sections
- mirrored or palindromic structures

### Loops and Recursion

Electronic music often makes mathematics audible through loops.

Small cells repeat, mutate, shift against each other and create larger emergent patterns.

## Signal Source Relevance

Mathematics matters here because it sits between structure and mystery.

In artists like Boards of Canada, Steve Reich, Philip Glass, Tim Hecker and William Basinski, repetition can feel mechanical, spiritual, emotional or uncanny depending on context.
