# Decay

Decay is one of the basic emotional textures of Signal Source.

Not decay as horror necessarily. More often it appears as a soft failure of things: tape losing clarity, colours fading, buildings aging, signals breaking up, memories becoming unreliable.

There is something about decay that makes art feel closer to memory.

Perfect things can feel dead. Damaged things often feel lived in.

## Why It Matters

Decay turns objects into time machines.

A clean recording tells you what happened.

A damaged recording tells you that time has passed.

That difference is huge. It is one of the reasons Boards of Canada, William Basinski, old VHS, public broadcasting and found photographs all sit so naturally together in this vault.

Decay is not just damage.

That is probably why it resonates so much here. It lets the past show physically on the surface of things.
