# Landscape

Landscape in Signal Source is rarely just scenery.

It is memory made visible.

A desert, a forest, a road, a field, a coast, a mountain, a town. These places carry atmosphere before they carry information. They tell you how to feel before they tell you what they mean.

Boards of Canada, Twin Peaks, Stalker, Anselm Kiefer and The Rings of Saturn all understand this in different ways.

## Emotional Geography

Some landscapes feel like escape.

Some feel like burial.

Some feel like a threshold.

Some feel like the future has already failed.

That is probably why landscape matters so much here. It externalises states of mind. Grief becomes coastline. Childhood becomes field. Paranoia becomes forest. Entropy becomes desert.

## Signal Source Reading

Landscape is one of the ways art avoids explanation.

Instead of saying what something means, it places you somewhere. If the place is strong enough, the meaning starts to form around you.
