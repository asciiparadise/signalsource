# Dissociation

## Why It Belongs Here

Dissociation belongs in Signal Source because many works in the vault show the self separating from itself.

A person becomes distant from their own body.

A memory feels unreal.

A life is watched from the outside.

An identity splits into another identity.

The world remains visible, but the connection to it weakens or changes.

That is why dissociation is important here. It explains a certain kind of cold, floating, unreal atmosphere that appears across Lynch, Pessoa, *House of Leaves*, trauma narratives and altered-state art.

## What We Actually Know

Dissociation is a psychological process involving disconnection from thoughts, feelings, memories, identity, body or surroundings.

It can occur in everyday mild forms, but also as a serious response to trauma or overwhelming stress.

This note should stay careful. Dissociation is not only an aesthetic mood. It can be a real and painful psychological condition.

## Signal Source Reading

Dissociation matters because it turns identity into distance.

The person is still there, but not fully inhabiting themselves in the ordinary way.

That distance can be protective. It can also become terrifying.

In art, dissociation often produces the feeling that reality has gone slightly silent. The world continues, but the self no longer fits cleanly inside it.
