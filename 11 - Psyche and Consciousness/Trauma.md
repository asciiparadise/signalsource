# Trauma

## Why It Belongs Here

Trauma belongs in Signal Source because many of the works here are not really about mystery in the normal sense.

They are about injury returning in symbolic form.

A scream. A loop. A house. A double. A lost child. A rewritten timeline. A damaged tape. A landscape that seems to remember something the people inside it cannot say.

That is why trauma matters here. It changes time. It changes memory. It changes identity. It makes the past behave as if it is still present.

## What We Actually Know

Trauma is a psychological and bodily response to overwhelming events or prolonged harmful conditions.

It can affect memory, emotion, perception, identity, sleep, relationships and the sense of time. Traumatic memory often does not behave like ordinary narrative memory. It can return through fragments, sensations, images, repetitions and sudden emotional states.

This note should stay careful. Trauma is a clinical and lived reality, not just an aesthetic.

## Signal Source Reading

Trauma matters because it explains why certain works refuse linear storytelling.

A clean plot can be dishonest when the subject itself is broken time.

Trauma repeats.

It hides.

It returns sideways.

It makes symbols do the work that ordinary language cannot do.
