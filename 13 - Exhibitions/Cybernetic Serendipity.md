# Cybernetic Serendipity

## Medium

Exhibition / cybernetics / computer art / systems art

## Evidence Status

**Documented historical exhibition.**

*Cybernetic Serendipity* was a landmark exhibition at the Institute of Contemporary Arts in London in 1968, curated by Jasia Reichardt.

It explored relationships between art, computers, cybernetics, machines, music, language, chance and systems.

## Why It Belongs Here

*Cybernetic Serendipity* belongs in Signal Source because it is one of the key historical moments where art, computation and systems thinking visibly touched.

The title is beautiful too.

Cybernetics suggests control, feedback, communication and machine logic.

Serendipity suggests accident, discovery, play and unexpected beauty.

That tension is exactly where much of Signal Source lives: pattern and chance, machine and intuition, structure and accident.

## What We Actually Know

The exhibition brought together artists, composers, engineers, mathematicians, scientists and technologists.

It included computer-generated images, machine-made poetry, interactive works, music, robotic systems and experiments with algorithmic or rule-based creation.

It is now often remembered as a major early public encounter between contemporary art and computing.

## Cybernetics as Art Language

Cybernetics is about feedback, control and communication in systems: machines, animals, organisations, brains, environments.

In art, this becomes powerful because the artwork no longer has to be a fixed object. It can behave. It can respond. It can generate. It can produce variation through rules.

## Signal Source Reading

*Cybernetic Serendipity* matters because it treats the machine not only as a tool, but as a collaborator in meaning.

That is still the live question now.

If a system can generate form, is the artwork the output, the rule, the interaction, or the human decision to frame it as art?

This is not just historical. It leads straight into contemporary generative art, AI image-making, procedural music, algorithmic composition and interactive systems.
