# Robert Beatty

## Type
Artist

## Medium
Visual Art / Illustration / Album Artwork / Graphic Design

## Core Idea

Robert Beatty belongs in Signal Source because his work sits between psychedelic print culture, airbrushed futurism, body-horror abstraction, surreal landscapes, retro technology and unstable perception.

His images often feel like diagrams from a world where biology, electronics, dreams and obsolete media have fused into one system.

## Why It Matters

Beatty is useful for the vault because he represents a contemporary visual language that feels connected to several Signal Source themes without simply imitating vintage aesthetics.

His work often suggests:

- altered consciousness
- warped anatomy
- analogue science fiction
- strange commercial graphics
- impossible surfaces
- synthetic nature
- psychedelic distortion
- visual signal corruption

## Atmosphere

Glossy, uncanny, synthetic, hallucinatory, retro-futurist, tactile, alien, humorous, unsettling.

## Relation to Music Culture

Beatty's work is strongly associated with music culture and album artwork, especially contemporary experimental, electronic, psychedelic and underground music.

For Signal Source, this makes him important not only as a visual artist, but as a bridge between listening and looking: a designer of worlds that feel like they could be heard.

## Vault Interpretation

Beatty's art often feels like a broadcast from an alternate commercial reality: part medical diagram, part record sleeve, part forgotten technology advert, part hallucination.

He belongs near Boards of Canada, not because the aesthetics are identical, but because both artists understand how obsolete visual and sonic languages can become portals into something stranger.
