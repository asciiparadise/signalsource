# Signal

## Core Meaning

Meaning carried across distance.

A signal can be a broadcast, a melody, a number, a voice, a heartbeat, a dream image, a memory, or a pattern that suddenly seems to organise the world.

## Why It Matters

Signal is one of the basic words of this vault.

I keep returning to the idea that art often behaves like a transmission. Something is sent, but not always clearly. It arrives damaged, delayed, distorted or incomplete. The receiver has to participate.

That is probably why Boards of Canada feel so central here. Their music often sounds like a signal that has travelled a long way before reaching us.

## Signal and Receiver

A signal only becomes meaningful when something receives it.

That seems obvious, but it matters. A radio tower, a tape, a television, a body, a dream, a listener — all of them can become receivers.

Signal Source is partly about that relationship.

The world transmits.

The mind interprets.
