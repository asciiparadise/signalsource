# Boards of Canada Hub

Boards of Canada sit very close to the centre of this vault.

I do not think that is only because I like the music. Plenty of music is good without becoming a world. Boards of Canada became a world. A place made from damaged memory, educational television, analogue machines, soft dread, nature, mathematics, childhood and the strange feeling that reality might have a pattern underneath it.

That is probably why they keep pulling so many other things into their orbit. Twin Peaks, hauntology, public broadcasting, sacred geometry, cult imagery, lost futures, family photographs, number stations, old synths, VHS haze. It all starts to feel related after a while. Maybe that is partly the danger of them. They make pattern recognition feel beautiful.

## Why They Matter Here

To me, Boards of Canada are not mainly about nostalgia.

Nostalgia is there, obviously. The old films, the children’s voices, the faded melodies. But underneath that, something more unstable is happening. Their music often feels like memory before it has become a story. A feeling without an explanation yet.

That is where the unease comes from.

The past is not safely behind us. It is inside the present, still transmitting.

## Questions That Keep Returning

- Why does their music feel remembered rather than composed?
- Why do simple melodies become uncanny when degraded?
- Why does childhood become frightening when viewed from a distance?
- How much of the mythology is intentional, and how much is created by listeners?
- Where does pattern recognition become belief?
- What happens when music becomes a hidden system?

## Signal Source Reading

My feeling is that Boards of Canada matter because they never fully explain the spell.

They provide fragments: numbers, symbols, voices, tape damage, landscapes, religious hints, old media, scientific language. Then they leave enough space for the listener to connect them.

That space is where the mythology lives.
