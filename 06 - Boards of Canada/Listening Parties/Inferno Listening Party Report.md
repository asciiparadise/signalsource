# Inferno Listening Party Report

## Type
Source / Event Report

## Work
[[Boards of Canada - Inferno]]

## Context

Report from a private listening party for Boards of Canada's *Inferno*. The sessions reportedly took place across several cities, each in a location with a distinct institutional or symbolic atmosphere: Paris, Berlin, Glasgow, New York, Los Angeles, London and Tokyo.

The Los Angeles session described here took place at Barnsdall Gallery Theatre, beneath Barnsdall Art Park, in a space connected to Frank Lloyd Wright architecture and described as bunker-like and brutalist.

## Key Observations

### Location as Ritual Frame

The listening party is described less like a normal album preview and more like an initiation space: underground entry, locked doors, fog, red light, candles, a hexagon-shaped fixture and a low physical throb beneath the room.

This matters because the setting seems designed to extend the album's symbolic language into physical space.

### Hexagon as Central Symbol

A rotating hexagon appears on screen before the album begins. It later becomes a window containing a looping flame. The report also mentions a hexagon-shaped lighting fixture.

The event reportedly opened with a distorted voice referencing a waiver and social experiments involving "audio triggers".

This connects strongly to Boards of Canada's long-standing interest in institutional language, psychological conditioning, educational media, broadcasts and controlled environments.

### Nursery Rhyme / Helter Skelter / Witch Imagery

A young girl's voice reportedly recites a foreboding nursery rhyme mentioning Helter Skelter, witches chanting, babies burning and that it would soon be "her turn".

This sits between childhood memory and cult horror: innocence used as a delivery mechanism for dread.

### Musical Character

The report describes the album as recognisably Boards of Canada, but also moving into new territory.

Noted elements:

- syncopated vocal samples
- dialogue fragments becoming rhythmic/percussive elements
- raw overdriven bass guitar
- sitar melodies
- possible 5/4 or 10/4 track
- possible harmonica
- tracks bleeding into one another
- four-on-the-floor rhythms
- reduced right-hand keyboard ornamentation compared with earlier material

### Emotional Reading

The writer pushes back against descriptions such as "demonic" or purely *Geogaddi*-like. Their reading is more nuanced: not demonic, not happy, but hopeful and tender.

This is important because it supports the idea that *Inferno* may use cultic, apocalyptic or dark religious material as an overlay for something more human: memory, family, grief, tenderness, endurance.

### You Retreat in Time and Space

The second-to-last track is described as a sudden return to pastoral family home-video imagery: road trips, children playing football, cookouts and the nostalgia-driven feeling of earlier Boards of Canada.

This appears to act as an emotional reveal or release after the darker ritual architecture of the event.

### Heartbeat Thread

The report identifies a heartbeat as the thread connecting the album: heart murmur, blood-rush sounds, possible EKG/heart monitor and a final heartbeat at the end.

This is one of the most important observations. It connects *Inferno* to mortality, embodiment, grief and life-signs. The heartbeat turns the album from abstract occult theatre into something bodily and human.
