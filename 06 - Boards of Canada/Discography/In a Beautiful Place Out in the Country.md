# In a Beautiful Place Out in the Country

## Artist

[[Boards of Canada Hub|Boards of Canada]]

## Release Type

EP

## Why It Matters

This release has always felt like one of the clearest examples of Boards of Canada making beauty feel slightly dangerous.

The title sounds peaceful, almost pastoral. A beautiful place, out in the country. It could be escape, nature, quietness, distance from the world. But the deeper you sit with it, the more that beauty starts to feel like withdrawal. Not just a retreat into landscape, but maybe into belief. Into a group. Into a place where the outside world has been rejected.

That is probably why it connects so strongly to cult imagery without needing to be obvious about it.

## The Cult Note

For me, this is the cult note in the Boards of Canada discography.

Not because it is lurid or sensational. It is much more subtle than that. The unease comes from the softness. From the idea that beauty and control might look similar from a distance.

A field, a community, a pastoral refuge, a voice, a belief system. It all feels calm until you realise there may be no easy way back out.

## Emotional Texture

The music feels open, but not free.

There is air around it, but also enclosure. It is beautiful in a way that feels slightly held in place. Almost like a memory of a utopia where something has already gone wrong.

This release matters because it shows how Boards of Canada can make the pastoral uncanny.
