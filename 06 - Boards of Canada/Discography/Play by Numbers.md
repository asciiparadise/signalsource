# Play by Numbers

## Artist

[[Boards of Canada Hub|Boards of Canada]]

## Release Type

Early tape / rare release

## Factual Status

**Status:** rare early release / mostly unavailable in ordinary listening contexts.

This note should keep a clear line between the known title and the interpretations attached to it. The title is important in fan memory, but the scarcity means this should not pretend to be a normal review.

## What We Actually Know

*Play by Numbers* belongs to the early Boards of Canada release prehistory and is usually grouped with the rare pre-*Twoism* tapes.

Specific claims about sound, track details or intent should be sourced before being treated as fact.

## What Is Mythology

The title itself has become meaningful because it fits so perfectly with later Boards of Canada concerns: childhood, counting, systems, instruction and hidden order.

That title-based reading is useful, but it is interpretive rather than documentary.

## Why It Matters

*Play by Numbers* might be one of the most perfectly named objects in the early Boards of Canada mythology.

Even before hearing anything, the title feels like it belongs: childhood, rules, counting, games, mathematics, instruction, hidden order. It sits exactly where Boards of Canada so often sit — between play and system.

That is probably why the title has such force in the archive.

## Childhood and Structure

The phrase “play by numbers” suggests something innocent and controlled at the same time.

Children play.

Systems count.

Boards of Canada are always moving between those two poles. The human and the mathematical. The soft and the coded. The nursery rhyme and the machine.
