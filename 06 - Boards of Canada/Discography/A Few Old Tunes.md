# A Few Old Tunes

## Artist

[[Boards of Canada Hub|Boards of Canada]]

## Release Type

Early tape / rare release

## Why It Matters

*A Few Old Tunes* feels less like a normal release and more like an attic box.

That is probably the best way I can describe it. It has that feeling of being full of fragments, sketches, strange little signals, half-formed memories, things that do not always announce themselves as important but still leave a trace.

It matters because it shows Boards of Canada before the world became fully organised.

The later records have architecture. This feels more like weather.

## The Archive Feeling

There is something moving about the looseness of the early material.

Not everything needs to be a statement. Some of it feels like a note left behind, a bit of tape, a melody tried once and then abandoned, an image that never became a larger scene.

That is part of why it belongs in Signal Source. It feels recovered rather than released.

## Lost Media and Fan Memory

The early tapes are surrounded by fan archaeology.

Copies, uploads, uncertain histories, tracklist debates, degraded quality, partial circulation. All of that becomes part of how the music is heard.

Maybe that is unfair in a strict musical sense, but for Boards of Canada it feels completely appropriate. Their music has always been about memory, and memory is never clean.

## Signal Source Reading

*A Few Old Tunes* matters because it makes the archive feel alive.

It is not polished mythology yet. It is the material before the myth hardens. And sometimes that kind of fragmentary state is more revealing than a finished album.
