# Hooper Bay

## Artist

[[Boards of Canada Hub|Boards of Canada]]

## Release Type

Early tape / rare release

## Why It Matters

*Hooper Bay* has that same strange quality as the other early tape titles: it feels like a place before it feels like a record.

A bay, a coastline, a remote edge, somewhere half-geographical and half-imagined. Even the name seems to open a landscape. That matters, because so much of Boards of Canada feels like music attached to places that are not fully real anymore.

Maybe they were real once.

Maybe they are only remembered.

## Place as Memory

The early BoC world often feels less like a sequence of compositions and more like a map of private locations.

Some are physical, some emotional, some maybe invented. *Hooper Bay* belongs to that category. It suggests geography, but also distance. A place you might not be able to return to. A name on an old tape. A location in the archive.

*Hooper Bay* matters because it shows how even a barely accessible object can still shape the mythology.

Sometimes the title, the absence and the imagined place become part of the music before the music is even heard.
