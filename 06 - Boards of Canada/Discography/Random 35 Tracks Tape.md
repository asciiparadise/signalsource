# Random 35 Tracks Tape

## Artist

[[Boards of Canada Hub|Boards of Canada]]

## Release Type

Early tape / rare release / fan-circulated archive

## Why It Matters

The title itself feels almost anti-mythological.

*Random 35 Tracks Tape*. Plain, functional, almost careless. But that plainness is part of the appeal. It sounds like something found rather than released, like a cassette with handwriting on it, passed along without the usual frame that tells you how to listen.

For Boards of Canada, that kind of object is never neutral.

The lack of framing becomes the frame.

## The Fan-Archaeology Object

This tape matters because of the way it circulates in memory and fan culture.

It is not just music. It is evidence. A recovered object. A fragment of a larger prehistory.

People listen partly for pleasure and partly for clues. Where did this idea go? Did this sound later become something else? Is this a sketch, a joke, a private experiment, or a lost key?

Maybe some of those questions are impossible to answer.

That is probably why they keep being asked.

## Signal Source Reading

*Random 35 Tracks Tape* sits right at the edge of archive and myth.

It is valuable not because it gives a clean origin story, but because it refuses one. It leaves the early Boards of Canada world messy, incomplete and alive.
