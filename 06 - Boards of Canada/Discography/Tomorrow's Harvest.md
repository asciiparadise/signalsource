# Tomorrow's Harvest

## Artist

[[Boards of Canada Hub|Boards of Canada]]

## Release Type

Studio album

## Why It Matters

*Tomorrow's Harvest* feels like the point where the future has already failed.

It is not apocalypse as explosion. It is apocalypse as atmosphere. Heat, dust, dead air, empty infrastructure, survival signals, bad news that has become weather.

After the warmth of *The Campfire Headphase*, this record feels like arriving somewhere abandoned.

## Entropy

This is the entropy record.

The old Boards of Canada elements are still there, but they have hardened. The childhood glow is almost gone. The public-broadcasting optimism has curdled into warning systems, disaster films and survivalist dread.

It feels less like memory and more like aftermath.

## Emotional Texture

The sound is dry, cinematic and exhausted.

Not dead exactly. More like a world still running after the people have left. Machines continue. Signals continue. The landscape continues. But the human promise behind them has collapsed.

## Signal Source Reading

If *Music Has the Right to Children* remembers a world of educational optimism, *Tomorrow's Harvest* imagines what happens after that optimism burns out.

The future did arrive.

It just arrived as warning, heat and empty space.
