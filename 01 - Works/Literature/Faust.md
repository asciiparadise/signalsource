# Faust

## Author

Johann Wolfgang von Goethe

## Medium

Drama / philosophical tragedy / pact narrative

## Why It Belongs Here

*Faust* belongs in Signal Source because it is one of the great works about forbidden striving.

Not forbidden knowledge in the simple horror sense. Something deeper than that. Faust wants more life, more knowledge, more experience, more power, more access to what lies beyond ordinary human limits.

That makes him a crucial figure for the vault.

He is not only a sinner or a magician. He is the modern human being turned into myth: restless, brilliant, dissatisfied, unable to accept the given world as enough.

## What We Actually Know

Goethe's *Faust* is a two-part dramatic work based on older Faust legends.

The central figure, Heinrich Faust, is a scholar who becomes dissatisfied with the limits of human knowledge and makes a pact with Mephistopheles.

The work combines theology, philosophy, folklore, satire, tragedy, erotic longing, metaphysics, politics and modernity.

## The Pact

The pact with Mephistopheles matters because it turns desire into structure.

Faust does not simply want pleasure. He wants total experience. He wants the world to open. That desire is recognisable, but also dangerous. It is the same impulse that drives occult experiments, forbidden books, scientific hubris and spiritual ambition.

This connects directly to [[The Ninth Gate]], [[Jack Parsons and the Babalon Working]], [[The Holy Mountain]], [[Dante - Inferno]] and [[The Occult]].

## Mephistopheles

Mephistopheles is not only a devil figure.

He is irony, negation, mockery, cleverness, corruption and worldly intelligence. He does not need to drag Faust into darkness by force. He offers movement, distraction and possibility.

That is more frightening.

The devil arrives not as pure evil, but as the permission to keep going without limit.

## Signal Source Reading

*Faust* matters because it asks whether the search for more can become damnation.

More knowledge.

More sensation.

More control.

More transformation.

More access.

That question runs through so much of Signal Source: occult books, hidden systems, scientific rupture, cults, conspiracy, cosmic ambition, even some forms of art-making.

The danger is not curiosity itself.

The danger is curiosity without humility.
