# Dream Logic

## Why It Belongs Here

Dream logic is one of the main languages of Signal Source.

It describes works where events do not follow ordinary causality, but still feel emotionally correct. A person becomes someone else. A room changes size. A voice comes from the wrong place. Time folds. A dead person speaks. A memory behaves like a place.

This can look irrational from the outside.

But dreams are not random in the way people sometimes think. They often have their own emotional order.

That is why dream logic matters here.

## What We Actually Know

Dream logic is not a formal scientific theory in the way a physics concept is. It is a descriptive term used in criticism, psychology and art to describe the associative, symbolic and unstable structures of dreams.

In dreams, identity, time, space and causality can shift quickly while still feeling meaningful to the dreamer.

Art can borrow that structure.

## Signal Source Reading

Dream logic is useful because it lets a work avoid the false neatness of plot.

Some experiences do not behave like stories.

Trauma does not.

Grief does not.

Memory does not.

Desire does not.

Dream logic gives form to those states without forcing them into ordinary explanation.

That is why Lynch is so central here. He does not simply make things weird. He understands that the unconscious has structure, but not the same structure as waking life.
