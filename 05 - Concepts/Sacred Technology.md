# Sacred Technology

## Why It Belongs Here

Sacred technology is one of the key concepts Signal Source needs, because so many works here treat machines as more than machines.

A radio is not only a radio.

A television is not only a television.

A tape machine is not only a recorder.

A synthesizer, antenna, camera, microphone, projector or computer can become a threshold object: something that turns invisible forces into experience.

That is where technology begins to feel sacred.

## What We Actually Know

This is an interpretive concept, not a fixed academic category.

It describes moments where technology takes on ritual, spiritual or mythic force. The device may be ordinary, but the way it functions in the work makes it feel like a mediator between worlds: human and non-human, visible and invisible, past and present, signal and meaning.

## Signal Source Reading

Sacred technology matters because modern culture did not get rid of magic.

It relocated it.

Older worlds had relics, temples, icons, bells, candles and ritual vessels. Modern worlds have screens, speakers, circuits, magnetic tape, radio towers and machines that let voices travel without bodies.

The feeling is not identical, but it rhymes.

A machine becomes sacred when it seems to carry more than information.

It carries presence.
