# Hidden Architecture

## Why It Belongs Here

Hidden architecture is the feeling that a work has a structure beneath its surface.

Not necessarily a literal secret code, though sometimes it can include that. More often it is the sense that the visible work is only the front layer of a deeper system: symbolic, musical, mathematical, psychological, historical or ritual.

Signal Source keeps returning to works that behave this way.

They feel built.

But not fully visible.

## What We Actually Know

This is an interpretive concept.

It describes the underlying structures that organise a work without always being directly explained: recurring numbers, mirrored scenes, symbolic patterns, spatial arrangements, formal proportions, repeated phrases, hidden references or emotional cycles.

## Signal Source Reading

Hidden architecture matters because it gives mystery form.

Without structure, mystery becomes vague.

With too much structure, mystery becomes a puzzle box.

The interesting works sit between those extremes. They suggest an order, but do not completely flatten themselves into explanation.

That is probably where Signal Source should stay too.
