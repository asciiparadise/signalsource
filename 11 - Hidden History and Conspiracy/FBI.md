# FBI

## Evidence Status

**Documented institution / domestic intelligence and law-enforcement history.**

The FBI is a real United States federal law-enforcement and domestic intelligence agency. Its history includes both ordinary criminal investigation and politically controversial surveillance, infiltration and counterintelligence activity.

As with the [[CIA]], the FBI should be treated carefully: documented history first, mythology second.

## Why It Belongs Here

The FBI belongs in Signal Source because it represents another form of hidden institutional power, but with a more domestic character.

Where the CIA often symbolises foreign covert action, the FBI often symbolises surveillance at home: files, informants, investigations, infiltration, moral panic, political monitoring and the strange intimacy of the state watching its own citizens.

That makes it relevant to the vault's interest in paranoia, hidden systems and cultural fear.

## What We Actually Know

The Federal Bureau of Investigation is the domestic intelligence and security service of the United States, as well as a federal law-enforcement agency.

Relevant historical areas include:

- organised crime investigations
- counterintelligence
- civil-rights-era surveillance
- COINTELPRO
- domestic extremism investigations
- political policing controversies
- files on artists, activists and public figures

COINTELPRO is especially important for Signal Source because it documents surveillance and disruption of political organisations and activists.

## What Became Mythology

The FBI has become part of the larger imagination of files, watchers, agents, black cars, informants and state suspicion.

In popular culture, the FBI can appear as protector, bureaucratic force, investigator, conspirator or unreliable gatekeeper of truth.

This is central to *Twin Peaks*, where the FBI is both rational investigative agency and doorway into impossible metaphysics.

## Signal Source Reading

The FBI matters because it turns investigation into atmosphere.

A file is not only a file.

It is a record of attention.

Someone watched. Someone wrote it down. Someone kept it. Someone redacted it.

That makes the FBI deeply connected to [[The Witness]], [[Archive]], [[State Secrecy]] and [[Pattern Recognition]].
