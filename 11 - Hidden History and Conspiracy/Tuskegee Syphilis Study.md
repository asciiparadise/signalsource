# Tuskegee Syphilis Study

## Evidence Status

**Documented medical ethics atrocity.**

The Tuskegee Syphilis Study is not conspiracy folklore.

It was a real U.S. Public Health Service study that ran from 1932 to 1972, involving Black men in Alabama who were misled and denied proper treatment while researchers observed the progression of untreated syphilis.

## Why It Belongs Here

Tuskegee belongs in Signal Source because it is one of the clearest examples of institutional betrayal.

It is not intelligence history in the same sense as [[MKUltra]] or [[COINTELPRO]], but it belongs beside them because it explains why distrust of official systems can have a factual basis.

When institutions do something this cruel, the damage does not end with the victims.

It damages public trust across generations.

## What We Actually Know

The study followed hundreds of Black men in Macon County, Alabama. Many had syphilis; others served as controls.

Participants were misled about their condition and the purpose of the study.

Even after penicillin became an effective standard treatment for syphilis, researchers did not properly treat the infected men.

The study was exposed publicly in 1972 and has since become one of the defining cases in modern medical ethics.

## What Is Mythology

Tuskegee does not need exaggeration.

The documented facts are already morally devastating.

But culturally, it has become larger than one study. It stands as a symbol of racialised medical abuse, state deception and the cost of treating human beings as research material rather than persons.
