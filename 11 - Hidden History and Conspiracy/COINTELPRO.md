# COINTELPRO

## Evidence Status

**Documented FBI programme.**

COINTELPRO should be treated as documented history, not folklore.

It was the FBI's Counterintelligence Program, active from the 1950s into the early 1970s, aimed at surveilling, infiltrating, discrediting and disrupting domestic political organisations and individuals.

## Why It Belongs Here

COINTELPRO belongs in Signal Source because it shows domestic state power operating through secrecy, files, infiltration and psychological pressure.

It is not as surreal as [[MKUltra]], but in some ways it is more directly political.

It shows how surveillance can move from observation into disruption.

That matters for the vault because it gives factual grounding to fears about institutions not only watching dissent, but shaping and damaging it.

## What We Actually Know

COINTELPRO targeted groups and individuals considered politically threatening by the FBI.

Targets included civil rights activists, Black liberation organisations, anti-war groups, socialist and communist groups, and other political movements.

Methods included:

- surveillance
- infiltration
- informants
- forged letters
- smear campaigns
- internal disruption
- psychological pressure
- attempts to discredit leaders

Martin Luther King Jr. was among the most significant targets of FBI surveillance and harassment.

## What Is Mythology

COINTELPRO became part of the broader mythology of domestic surveillance because the documented reality is already deeply disturbing.

The mythology often expands into the idea that all political opposition is secretly managed or destroyed from within. That can become too totalising.

But the factual history means suspicion of domestic intelligence power is not irrational by default.

## Signal Source Reading

COINTELPRO matters because it turns politics into invisible theatre.

The public sees movements, leaders, conflicts and scandals.

Behind the scenes, informants, forged documents and pressure campaigns distort the social field.

This connects to one of Signal Source's central anxieties: the visible surface of history may not be the whole structure.
