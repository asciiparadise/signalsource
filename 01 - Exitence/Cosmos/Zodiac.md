# Zodiac

## Evidence Status

**Symbolic / astronomical-historical system.**

The zodiac has a real astronomical basis as a band of sky along the ecliptic, but its astrological interpretation is symbolic rather than scientific.

Signal Source should keep both layers visible.

## Why It Belongs Here

The zodiac belongs in Signal Source because it is a circular system for turning sky, time and identity into symbols.

That alone makes it important.

It is geometry, astronomy, mythology, seasonality and personality language all folded into one wheel. The zodiac is not just twelve signs. It is a model of recurrence: cycles, returns, oppositions, houses, seasons, birth, fate and pattern.

The sky becomes a symbolic clock.

## What We Actually Know

In astronomy, the zodiac refers to the region of the sky through which the Sun, Moon and planets appear to move from Earth's perspective.

In Western astrology, the zodiac is divided into twelve signs:

- Aries
- Taurus
- Gemini
- Cancer
- Leo
- Virgo
- Libra
- Scorpio
- Sagittarius
- Capricorn
- Aquarius
- Pisces

These signs are used symbolically in astrological charts, often connected to elements, modalities, planets and archetypal qualities.

## Why It Matters Symbolically

The zodiac is powerful because it gives time a shape.

A year becomes a circle.

A birth moment becomes a map.

A personality becomes a constellation of tensions.

Again, this should not be treated as scientific proof. But symbolically, it is one of the most enduring systems humans have made for linking cosmic order to inner life.

## Signal Source Reading

The zodiac matters because it shows pattern recognition at cosmic scale.

Humans looked at the sky, saw recurring movement, and built a symbolic machine from it.

That machine still works culturally even for people who do not literally believe in it. It gives language to moods, archetypes, cycles and identity.

In Signal Source, the zodiac connects to both the danger and beauty of symbolic systems.

It can deepen attention.

It can also explain too much.
