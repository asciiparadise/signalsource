# Black Holes

## Why They Belong Here

Black holes belong in Signal Source because they are scientific objects that already feel mythological.

They are real astrophysical phenomena, not symbols invented by artists. But the moment we begin thinking about them, they seem to pull symbolic meaning toward themselves: void, threshold, collapse, hidden information, time distortion, gravity, disappearance.

That is probably why they fit the vault so naturally.

They are where physics starts to feel like metaphysics.

## What We Actually Know

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape beyond the event horizon.

Black holes can form when massive stars collapse, and they also exist at the centres of many galaxies as supermassive black holes.

Important concepts:

- event horizon
- singularity, in classical general relativity
- gravitational collapse
- time dilation
- accretion disk
- Hawking radiation, theoretically
- information paradox

This note should stay careful. Black holes are heavily studied scientific objects, but some popular descriptions over-mythologise them.

## Time and Memory

Black holes also matter because of time.

Near a black hole, time behaves differently relative to distant observers. This is not just poetic. It is part of relativity.

That makes black holes useful for thinking about the vault's larger time obsession: memory, loops, delay, impossible return, and the feeling that some moments have more gravitational pull than others.

## Signal Source Reading

A black hole is what happens when a thing becomes so dense it turns into a structure of absence.

That sounds like physics, but also like trauma.

A memory can behave that way.

A historical event can behave that way.

A missing object can behave that way.

Everything around it bends.
