# The Witness

## Why It Belongs Here

The witness belongs in Signal Source because so many works here are built around seeing, recording, remembering or failing to speak.

A witness is not only a person who observes an event.

A witness can be a camera, a tape, a photograph, a document, a landscape, a ruined building, a body, a survivor, a dream, or even a work of art itself.

That matters because the vault keeps returning to things that carry evidence without fully explaining it.

## What We Actually Know

This is an interpretive concept.

It describes the role of witnessing in art, memory and history: the person or object that remains after an event and carries its trace forward.

Witnessing is especially important in works dealing with trauma, atrocity, disappearance, grief and historical violence.

## Signal Source Reading

The witness matters because it resists disappearance.

Something happened.

Someone saw.

Something recorded.

Something remains.

But the witness does not always make the truth simple. Sometimes it makes truth more painful, because the evidence survives without repair.
