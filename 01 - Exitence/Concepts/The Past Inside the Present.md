# The Past Inside the Present

## Why It Belongs Here

The past inside the present is probably one of the central feelings of Signal Source.

It describes the moment where history does not feel behind us. It is still active, still leaking through objects, buildings, sounds, habits, photographs, ruins, family patterns, political structures and old media.

This matters because many works in the vault are not simply nostalgic.

They are haunted by time that has not finished happening.

## What We Actually Know

This is an interpretive concept rather than a formal historical theory.

It overlaps with ideas like memory, hauntology, trauma, inheritance and historical recurrence. It is useful because it describes how cultural and personal pasts continue to shape the present without always being visible.

## Signal Source Reading

The past inside the present matters because it changes how memory works.

Memory is not only something a person carries privately.

It can be built into a road, a broadcast, a record sleeve, a family house, a national myth, a failed institution, a coastline, a ruined building.

The present is never clean.

It is layered.
