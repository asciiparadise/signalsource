# The Shadow

## Why It Belongs Here

The Shadow is one of the most useful ideas in the whole vault.

In Jungian terms, the shadow is the part of the self that is rejected, denied, hidden or pushed out of conscious identity. It is not automatically evil. That is important. It can contain shame, aggression and fear, but also energy, truth and unrealised parts of the person.

But when the shadow is denied completely, it becomes dangerous.

That is where it becomes relevant to art.

## What We Actually Know

The concept comes mainly from [[Carl Jung]] and analytical psychology.

The shadow describes the unconscious material a person or culture does not want to identify with.

On an individual level, it can appear in dreams, projections, compulsions or irrational reactions.

On a cultural level, it can appear through monsters, enemies, doubles, scapegoats, taboo symbols and recurring myths.

## Signal Source Reading

The Shadow matters because it explains why some works feel frightening even when nothing overtly horrific is happening.

Something denied is returning.

A family secret.

A national crime.

A private guilt.

A desire that cannot be admitted.

A memory that refuses to stay buried.

The shadow is not simply darkness. It is the cost of pretending there is no darkness.
