# Dreams

## Why They Belong Here

Dreams belong in Signal Source because they are one of the oldest and strangest forms of meaning-making.

They are private, but they often feel larger than the person having them. They are irrational, but not meaningless. They use images, substitutions, repetitions, impossible spaces and emotional logic. They can feel like messages, even when we are not sure who or what is sending them.

That uncertainty is exactly why they matter here.

## What We Actually Know

Dreaming is a normal part of sleep and consciousness.

Scientifically, dreams are connected to sleep cycles, memory, emotion, imagery, neural activity and psychological processing.

Culturally, dreams have been treated as prophecy, divine communication, unconscious material, symbolic theatre, trauma replay, random neural noise, and imaginative composition.

Signal Source should not reduce dreams to only one explanation.

## Signal Source Reading

Dreams matter because they show that meaning does not always need ordinary causality.

A room can be a person.

A road can be guilt.

A voice can be memory.

A dead person can return as a question.

Dreams turn the psyche into cinema before cinema even existed.
