# Societas x Tape

## Artist

[[Boards of Canada Hub|Boards of Canada]]

## Release Type

Mix / broadcast / influence map

## Why It Matters

*Societas x Tape* may be one of the most revealing Boards of Canada objects, even though it is not a normal album.

It feels like a window opened sideways into their record collection, their memory bank, their private weather system. Not an explanation exactly. They would never give us that. But a kind of map made from other people's music.

Maybe that is why it matters so much. It shows the soil rather than the flower.

## Influence as Atmosphere

The tape is not just a playlist.

It feels curated as a world. Strange folk, library music, electronic fragments, psychedelia, odd transmissions, old textures, things that sound half-lost before they even begin.

Listening to it, I get the feeling that Boards of Canada are less interested in genre than in temperature. Does something have the right age? The right dust? The right sadness? The right strangeness?

## Fan Archaeology

This is also a major object for fan archaeology.

People identify tracks, chase sources, reconstruct references, and use the tape almost like an index to the Boards of Canada unconscious.

That is probably the correct way to treat it.

Not as a key that unlocks everything, but as a room full of objects that explains the smell of the house.

## Signal Source Reading

*Societas x Tape* is probably the closest thing to an influence map they have ever given us.

But even here, the map is foggy.

That is what makes it useful. It does not reduce the mystery. It gives the mystery more texture.
