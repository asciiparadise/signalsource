# The Final Dossier and the Rewritten Timeline

## Core Idea

The ending of *The Final Dossier* is one of the most quietly horrifying parts of the expanded *Twin Peaks* mythology.

It asks what happens when Cooper changes the past.

The answer is not clean. The world does not simply correct itself. It begins rewriting memory, evidence and identity from the inside.

## Laura Palmer Missing

After Cooper intercepts Laura in the woods, the old timeline begins to disappear.

In the new history, Laura was not found dead on the beach.

She went missing.

That sounds like a softer outcome, but it is not. The murder is erased, the investigation changes, the town's memory shifts, and the original wound is buried under a different wound.

Cooper has not healed the past.

He has destabilised it.

## Tammy Preston's Double Memory

The most frightening part is Tammy Preston's experience.

For a short time, she can feel both realities in her mind at once. She remembers Laura's murder, but new memories are arriving, pushing the old ones out.

That is such a strong *Twin Peaks* idea.

Memory is not a stable archive. It can be invaded. Rewritten. Overlaid. Corrupted like a tape.

## Writing Before Forgetting

Tammy writes the dossier partly as resistance.

She is trying to record the old truth before the new reality overwrites her completely. This turns the book itself into an emergency object.

Not just a report.

A memory anchor.

## Signal Source Reading

This is where Frost makes time feel fragile.

Not abstractly fragile, but personally fragile. If reality changes and your memory changes with it, what can you trust?

A newspaper? A dossier? A dream? A scream?

Maybe that is why the ending hurts so much. Cooper wanted to save Laura, but he ends up attacking the very structure that allowed anyone to remember her truth.
