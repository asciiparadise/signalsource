# My Reflective Style

## Purpose

This is the house style for Signal Source.

The vault should not sound like Wikipedia, a museum label, or a formal academic essay. It should sound like a person trying to understand why something matters.

## Core Description

The style is personal first, analytical second.

It often begins with a reaction, instinct, memory, taste, or emotional response, then widens into interpretation. It is allowed to be uncertain. It does not need to close every thought down.

The voice should feel human, direct, slightly informal, emotionally honest and interpretive.

## Movement

A useful pattern:

personal reaction → wider interpretation → uncertainty → open-ended conclusion

## Useful Phrases

- I have to admit...
- To me...
- My feeling is...
- Maybe I am biased, who knows?
- It feels like...
- There is something about...
- I might be reading too much into it...
- It does not tell the story in a straight line.
- Something is seeping in around the edges.

## What To Avoid

Avoid:

- overly academic phrasing
- corporate language
- marketing tone
- too much certainty
- encyclopedia voice
- generic summaries
- polished critic-speak

## Better Example

I do not think the album is only about cults. That is the obvious surface. Underneath it, I think there is something more personal going on. Something about loss, faith, fear, and the body slowly failing.

## Worse Example

The album utilises cult imagery as a conceptual framework through which the artists interrogate mortality, religion, and metaphysical uncertainty.

## Rule for Signal Source Notes

Every note should try to answer:

- Why does this matter to me?
- What does it feel like?
- What does it connect to?
- What might it be doing symbolically?

The vault is allowed to think in public.
