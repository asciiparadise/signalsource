# Desert

## Core Meaning

Emptiness, exposure, heat, revelation, survival, failed futures.

## Why It Matters

The desert is one of those landscapes that immediately changes the emotional temperature of a work.

It strips things down. There is nowhere to hide. Buildings look abandoned faster. Technology looks more absurd. Signals feel weaker and more mysterious. A road through a forest suggests myth. A road through a desert suggests aftermath.

That is probably why it fits so naturally with *Tomorrow's Harvest* and *Reach for the Dead*.

## Desert as Aftermath

In Signal Source, the desert is not only a place.

It often feels like what remains after the future has burned out.

Heat, distance, silence, dust, military traces, radio towers, survival infrastructure, dead air. It can be beautiful, but it rarely feels comforting.

## Signal Source Reading

The desert feels like memory after the people have left.

Not dead exactly. More like emptied out. A place where the world keeps transmitting, but nobody is sure who is listening.
