# Radio Tower

## Core Meaning

Transmission, distance, surveillance, isolation, hidden communication.

## Why It Matters

A radio tower is never just infrastructure in Signal Source.

It suggests that something is being sent or received, even if we do not know by whom. It turns landscape into signal-space. A field, a desert or a hill becomes charged because a tower is standing there, quietly broadcasting into the air.

That is probably why radio towers feel so connected to Boards of Canada, number stations, Cold War secrecy and abandoned places.

## Transmission and Loneliness

There is something lonely about radio technology.

The signal travels, but the sender and receiver may never meet. A voice crosses distance. A code repeats. A broadcast continues long after its context has been lost.

That loneliness is part of the atmosphere.
