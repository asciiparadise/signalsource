# Modern Art Canon

## Purpose

A curated constellation of works that repeatedly return to the core concerns of Signal Source:

- memory
- time
- mortality
- hidden systems
- spirituality
- childhood
- apocalypse
- identity
- liminal spaces
- the past inside the present

---

# Music

## The Disintegration Loops — William Basinski

Tape loops physically disintegrating during playback. One of the defining artistic statements on memory, decay, loss and time. The medium becomes the message.

Connections:
- Memory
- Signal and Noise
- Decay
- Mortality

## Virgins — Tim Hecker

Dense, overwhelming and architectural. Sound as landscape rather than composition.

Connections:
- Liminal Places
- The Sublime
- Signal and Noise

## Promises — Floating Points, Pharoah Sanders, London Symphony Orchestra

Repetition used as a spiritual device. Tiny changes become emotionally immense.

Connections:
- Spirituality
- Time
- Sacred Minimalism

## Ravedeath, 1972 — Tim Hecker

Feels like wandering through a ruined cathedral made of memory and distortion.

Connections:
- Ruins
- Memory
- Spirituality

# Film

## The Tree of Life

Childhood, death, God, memory and the cosmos held together in a single work.

Connections:
- Memory
- Childhood
- Spirituality
- Mortality

## Stalker

Perhaps the foundational text for modern liminal art. A journey through a forbidden zone that becomes a journey through consciousness.

Connections:
- Liminal Places
- Faith
- Mystery
- Hidden Systems

## Uncle Boonmee Who Can Recall His Past Lives

Dreams, reincarnation and memory treated as ordinary reality.

Connections:
- Memory
- Spirituality
- The Uncanny

## Memoria

A mysterious sound becomes a meditation on memory itself.

Connections:
- Sound
- Memory
- Signal and Noise

## Synecdoche, New York

Identity, mortality, art and the impossibility of fully understanding one's own life.

Connections:
- Mortality
- Identity
- Time

## One Battle After Another

History, inheritance, political memory and unfinished struggles passed between generations.

Connections:
- Memory
- History
- Myth

## The House That Jack Built

Art, evil, self-mythology and the danger of aestheticising destruction.

Connections:
- The Occult
- Myth
- Art and Morality

# Television

## The Leftovers

Begins as a mystery and becomes an exploration of grief, faith and survival.

## Dekalog

Ethics, spirituality and modern life examined through ten interconnected stories.

## Atlanta

Surreal, unsettling and culturally insightful. Reality often bends without explanation.

## Too Old to Die Young

Hypnotic, symbolic and dreamlike. More ritual than narrative.

## House of Leaves

A labyrinth disguised as a novel.

## 2666

A vast work haunted by invisible forces.

## The Book of Disquiet

One of the greatest explorations of consciousness ever written.

## The Road

Minimal surface, profound depth. Love and mortality stripped to essentials.

# Visual Art

## Anselm Kiefer

History, mythology, religion, memory and ruins.

If Inferno existed as paintings, some of them would resemble Kiefer.

## Christina's World

Quietly uncanny Americana. Memory, distance and longing.

## Gerhard Richter

Blurred paintings where memory and reality dissolve into one another.

## Zdzislaw Beksinski

Dreamlike landscapes that feel remembered rather than imagined.

---

# Central Observation

Most works in this canon are not primarily about plot.

They are about what remains:

- ruins
- memories
- ghosts
- symbols
- echoes
- unfinished histories
- hidden structures

This places them in direct conversation with Boards of Canada, Twin Peaks: The Return and the wider Signal Source project.
